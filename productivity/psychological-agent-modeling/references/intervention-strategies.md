# Schema Intervention Strategies (Session-Derived)

> Condensed from 2026-06-21 session. Full version: ~/projects/psychology-assessment/图式深度分析与改变执行手册.md

## Emotional Inhibition (Score: 5.33/7)

**Formation**: Emotional expression punished/ignored; rewarded for being "mature"/"rational"; family environment where emotions were dangerous.

**Maintenance cycle**: Trigger → body signal → schema alarm "can't lose control" → intellectualization/distraction/rationalization/isolation → emotion suppressed → short-term relief → long-term cost accumulates.

**Four strategies**:
1. **Body awareness** (5min/day): Body scan from head to toe, notice sensations without naming emotions, stay with each sensation 30 seconds.
2. **Emotion naming** (daily): "Today I felt ____, intensity ____, in situation ____." Use basic emotion word bank (happy/sad/angry/scared/disgusted/surprised → advance to lonely/ashamed/jealous/conflicted).
3. **Graduated emotional exposure** (weekly): Week 1: write one honest feeling in private journal → Week 2: tell safe friend "I'm not doing great" → Week 3: express small dissatisfaction → Week 4: express positive emotion → Week 5: stay present when feeling vulnerable → Week 6: say "I'm scared" or "I need you."
4. **Cognitive defusion** (ACT): "I notice I'm analyzing this. I notice I'm using thinking to avoid feeling." Don't stop analyzing — just notice you're doing it.

## Disconnection & Rejection Domain (Score: 4.17/7)

**Core belief**: "I'm not good enough, so people will leave me. Even if they stay, they won't truly understand me."

**Key sub-schemas**:
- Abandonment (4.33): "The people I love will eventually leave."
- Emotional deprivation (4.33): "No one will truly understand me or meet my emotional needs." Three forms: nurturance, empathy, protection deprivation.
- Mistrust (4.0): "Others will use me, hurt me, betray me." — cognitive core of avoidant attachment.
- Defectiveness (4.0): "There's something fundamentally wrong with me." + Unrelenting Standards = "I must be perfect to compensate for my defect."

**Fatal cycle**: Emotional inhibition → don't express needs → partner doesn't know what you need → not met → deprivation intensifies → even less willing to express → cycle deepens.

**Three strategies**:
1. **Old script vs present reality**: Trigger → old script → evidence for/against → alternative interpretation. Don't need to believe the alternative — just see the old script isn't the only possible reading.
2. **Corrective emotional experience**: Identify safe person → micro trust tests (share low-risk info → medium-risk → real feeling) → record whether old script came true.
3. **Internal safe base**: Visualization of a safe place + ideal attachment figure saying "You don't need to be perfect to be loved. You don't need to hide to be safe. You can stay."

## Unrelenting Standards (Score: 4.67/7)

**Core belief**: "If I don't do my best, I have no value."

**Formation**: Conditional love (praise for achievement, disappointment for failure); only achievement recognized.

**Three strategies**:
1. **Distinguish healthy standards vs harsh standards**: "I want to do well" vs "I must do my best." Can relax vs relaxing = guilt.
2. **Deliberate imperfection experiments**: Week 1: submit 70% work → Week 2: send unedited message → Week 3: say "I don't know" → Week 4: do something you've been afraid to fail at. Record: what did my schema predict? What actually happened?
3. **Internal value anchoring**: Write daily: "Today I don't need to do anything to prove my value. As a human being, I have value inherently."

## Daily Practice Protocol

- **Morning (3min)**: What schema trigger might I encounter today? What alternative response can I use?
- **Throughout day**: When emotion gets suppressed (body signals: tight chest, throat lump, stiff shoulders), pause 30 seconds, ask "what am I feeling right now?"
- **Evening (5min)**: Body scan (2min) + emotion thermometer (1min) + schema awareness (2min)
- **Weekly (30min)**: Review emotion journal for patterns.

## Red Flags for Professional Help

- Strong panic or dissociation during emotion exercises
- Repeated failure at graduated exposure with declining self-worth
- Using more extreme emotion suppression (substance abuse, self-harm, binge eating)
- Sleep/appetite/daily functioning significantly impaired
- No images emerge from safe base visualization (only darkness/void)
- Persistent hopelessness or meaninglessness

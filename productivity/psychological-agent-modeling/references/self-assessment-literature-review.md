# Academic Evidence Summary (2020-2025)

## IFS (Internal Family Systems)
- Hodgdon et al. (2021) *J Clinical Psychology*: Systematic review, 18 studies, d=0.52-0.81
- Haddock et al. (2023) *Psychotherapy Research*: Meta-analysis, 12 RCTs, g=0.78 vs waitlist
- Shadick et al. (2023) *J Rheumatology*: RCT for RA pain + depression
- Criticisms: "parts" model lacks independent empirical validation; mechanism research weak

## Schema Therapy
- Bamelis et al. (2014) *JAMA Psychiatry*: N=323, recovery 81% vs 37% TAU — landmark RCT
- Firth et al. (2023) *Behaviour Research and Therapy*: Updated meta-analysis, robust across outcomes
- Janssen et al. (2023) *Clinical Psychology Review*: Schemas are modifiable, change mediates outcomes
- Bach et al. (2018-2022): Disconnection/Rejection domain strongest predictor of interpersonal dysfunction
- Pepping et al. (2013, 2021): Insecure attachment maps onto specific schema domains

## Attachment Theory
- Candel & Turliuc (2023) *PAID*: 148-study meta-analysis, anxious + avoidant ↓ relationship satisfaction
- Li & Chan (2021) *EJSP*: Anxious → jealousy/conflict; Avoidant → low intimacy; moderated by culture
- Hudson et al. (2023) *J Marital and Family Therapy*: EFT meta-analysis, d≈0.70-1.0
- Mikulincer & Shaver (2022): 3rd edition textbook, integrated neuroscience evidence

## ACT
- Lang et al. (2023) *J Traumatic Stress*: 15 RCTs, moderate effect for PTSD, comparable to CBT
- Kashdan & McKnight (2022): Psychological flexibility + values → healthier identity resolution
- Halliburton & Cooper (2021) *J Contextual Behavioral Science*: Review of ACT for identity distress
- Wharton et al. (2023) *Behavior Therapy*: ACT for moral injury + PTSD, self-as-context as key mechanism

## Polyvagal Theory — WARNING
- Montgomery et al. (2022) *Frontiers in Psychology*: Core claims NOT well-supported empirically
- Beauchaine (2023) *Psychophysiology*: Neurobiological mechanisms poorly specified
- Phylogenetic hierarchy rejected by comparative neuroanatomists
- "Neuroception" lacks operational definition
- Use as clinical metaphor ONLY

## MBTI — WARNING
- Does not meet psychometric standards (reliability/validity)
- Not recognized by mainstream academic psychology as scientific tool
- Useful as casual self-reflection language system only

# Academic Papers on Body Recomposition & Posture Correction

## Somatotype Validity

### Paper 1
- **Title**: Genetics of Somatotype and Physical Fitness in Children and Adolescents
- **Authors**: Silventoinen K, Maia J, Jelenkovic A, et al.
- **Year**: 2021
- **Journal**: *American Journal of Human Biology* (IF: 2.0)
- **Finding**: Twin study shows somatotype heritability 0.65-0.91, indicating genetics dominate over environment in body type determination.

### Paper 2
- **Title**: Prediction of Somatotype from Bioimpedance Analysis in Elite Youth Soccer Players
- **Authors**: Campa F, Matias CN, Nikolaidis PT, et al.
- **Year**: 2020
- **Journal**: *Int J Environ Res Public Health* (IF: 4.6)
- **Finding**: BIA can objectively predict somatotype categories, but somatotype's value for training prescription differentiation is limited.

### Paper 3
- **Title**: Relationship of Body Composition and Somatotype with Physical Activity Level and Nutrition Knowledge in Elite and Non-Elite Orienteering Athletes
- **Authors**: Esteve-Ibáñez H, Drehmer E, da Silva VS, et al.
- **Year**: 2025
- **Journal**: *Nutrients* (IF: 5.9)
- **Finding**: Elite athletes cluster toward mesomorph body type, reflecting sport selection rather than differential training adaptation.

## Posture Correction (Upper Crossed Syndrome)

### Paper 1 — Key Meta-Analysis
- **Title**: The Effect of Various Therapeutic Exercises on Forward Head Posture, Rounded Shoulder, and Hyperkyphosis Among People with Upper Crossed Syndrome: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis
- **Authors**: Sepehri S, Sheikhhoseini R, Piri H, Sayyadi P
- **Year**: 2024
- **Journal**: *BMC Musculoskeletal Disorders* (IF: 2.3)
- **Finding**: 22-study meta-analysis — combined strength + stretch + shoulder program most effective. FHP improvement: 95%CI -1.85 to -1.16.

### Paper 2
- **Title**: Comparative Effectiveness of Isometric and Isotonic Exercises on Hyperkyphosis, Forward Head Posture, and Rounded Shoulders
- **Authors**: Khaledi A, Minoonejad H
- **Year**: 2026
- **Journal**: *Archives of Bone and Joint Surgery*
- **Finding**: 8-week RCT — both isometric and isotonic improve FHP (-6.79° vs -7.97°), RS (-6.44° vs -8.09°), hyperkyphosis (-7.21° vs -7.39°). Isotonic slightly better, no significant difference.

### Paper 3
- **Title**: Effects of Telerehabilitation Combining Diaphragmatic Breathing Re-Education and Shoulder Stabilization Exercises on Neck Pain, Posture, and Function in Young Adult Men with Upper Crossed Syndrome
- **Authors**: Jeong GH, Lee BH
- **Year**: 2024
- **Journal**: *Journal of Clinical Medicine* (IF: 3.9)
- **Finding**: Telerehabilitation mode effective; diaphragmatic breathing retraining adds benefit to shoulder stabilization for UCS.

## Body Recomposition (Simultaneous Fat Loss + Muscle Gain)

### Paper 1 — Landmark Study
- **Title**: Higher Compared with Lower Dietary Protein During an Energy Deficit Combined with Intense Exercise Promotes Greater Lean Mass Gain and Fat Mass Loss: A Randomized Trial
- **Authors**: Longland TM, Oikawa SY, Mitchell CJ, Devries MC, Phillips SM
- **Year**: 2016
- **Journal**: *American Journal of Clinical Nutrition* (IF: 7.1)
- **Finding**: At ~40% deficit + intense exercise for 4 weeks, 2.4 g/kg/d protein group gained +1.2 kg LBM and lost -4.8 kg fat, vs 1.2 g/kg group: +0.1 kg LBM, -3.5 kg fat. First rigorous proof of simultaneous recomposition.

### Paper 2 — ISSN Position Stand
- **Title**: International Society of Sports Nutrition Position Stand: Diets and Body Composition
- **Authors**: Aragon AA, Schoenfeld BJ, et al. (17 experts)
- **Year**: 2017
- **Journal**: *J Int Soc Sports Nutr* (IF: 4.5)
- **Finding**: 2.3-3.1 g/kg LBM protein optimal during deficit. Higher baseline body fat → more aggressive deficit tolerated. Slower weight loss preserves more LBM in leaner individuals.

### Paper 3 — Recent Confirmation
- **Title**: Comparison of Two Nutritional Protocols in Body Re-composition of Resistance-Trained Participants
- **Authors**: Vargas-Molina S, García-Palumbo A, García-Sillero M, Bonilla DA, et al.
- **Year**: 2026
- **Journal**: *European Journal of Applied Physiology* (IF: 3.0)
- **Finding**: 10-week RCT at 2.5 g/kg/d protein — both moderate deficit (-2.94 kg fat, +1.04 kg LBM) and eucaloric (-1.41 kg fat, +0.97 kg LBM) groups achieved recomposition, challenging traditional energy balance model.
